Manga is Japanese comics or graphic stories that combine text and illustrations to convey plot, emotions, and atmosphere. It developed as a distinct art form in Japan and today is one of the most recognizable elements of modern global pop culture.

Unlike Western comics, manga has its own artistic and narrative characteristics. The artwork is often created in black and white, which allows artists to produce large volumes of content more quickly. Great attention is given to characters’ emotions: facial expressions, symbolic visual effects, changing camera angles, and panel pacing help convey the mood of each scene more deeply.

Manga is not aimed only at children — it is divided into age and thematic categories:

- Shōnen — adventure, action, and character growth (for teenage boys),
- Shōjo — romance and emotional relationships,
- Seinen — more complex stories for adult audiences,
- Josei — realistic stories about everyday life and relationships,
- Kodomo — manga for children.

One distinctive feature of manga is its right-to-left reading direction, which preserves the original Japanese page structure. Stories are usually first published in serialized form in specialized magazines and, if successful, later released as collected volumes called tankōbon.

Manga has a strong influence on other media. Popular series often receive anime adaptations, live-action films, video games, merchandise, and even theatrical productions. Many modern artists and writers around the world draw inspiration from manga due to its cinematic storytelling style.

In Ukraine, manga is actively growing and gradually becoming an important part of modern pop culture. The number of official Ukrainian translations is increasing, and themed communities, festivals, and stores dedicated to Japanese culture are opening across the country. Ukrainian publishers such as Nasha Idea, Molfar Comics, and Lantsuta Publisher play a significant role in popularizing manga by releasing well-known series in Ukrainian. As a result, manga is becoming more accessible to readers, and interest in Japanese culture among young people in Ukraine continues to grow.
